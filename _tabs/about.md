I write about the psychology of knowing yourself — and what gets in the way.

My background is in data science and counseling psychology. I spent years inside formulas that looked right from the outside and felt hollow on the inside. That gap is where most of my writing comes from.

The books I write aren't self-help in the usual sense. There's no positivity theater, no instructions to journal your way to a better morning routine. What I'm interested in is the honest version: why people lose themselves, how that actually happens psychologically, and what it takes to build something more solid underneath.

**The Self You've Been Ignoring** is the first volume — about self-awareness, approval, and learning to be alone without it feeling like failure. The second volume is about romantic relationships: why smart people disappear into them, and how not to.

If something here resonates, it's probably because you've already suspected what I'm writing about is true. You just hadn't seen it said directly yet.
