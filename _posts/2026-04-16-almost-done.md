I keep waiting for it to feel like something.

The manuscript is done — or close enough to done that the remaining work is detail work, not thinking work. Nine chapters, an epilogue, references. Somewhere around fifty thousand words. I've been working on this for months, and now it just sits there in a folder on my desktop like any other file.

I don't feel relieved. I don't feel proud, exactly. It's more like that strange flatness you get after something you've been carrying for a long time is finally set down. Your arms don't know what to do.

---

The book is called *The Self You've Been Ignoring*. It's about self-awareness — specifically about all the ways people lose track of who they actually are, and what it takes to find that again. Not in a motivational-poster way. More in a here's-what-the-psychology-actually-says way, combined with the parts of my own life I'm willing to put on the page.

I've been studying psychology for years — formally and informally — and one thing I kept noticing is that there's a gap between the research and the people who actually need it. The concepts that would genuinely help people aren't hard to understand. They're just buried in academic language, or packaged in self-help books that are more interested in making you feel good than telling you something true.

That's what I was trying to close.

---

I started writing this after a period in my life where I had more time alone than I'd had in years. Post-divorce, between certain things. For a long time that kind of quiet felt uncomfortable. Then at some point it started to feel like room.

That's more or less where Chapter 1 came from. The idea that being alone isn't a problem to solve — it's a skill you either build or you don't.

---

What comes next is editing, cover design, figuring out publishing logistics. And then starting Volume Two, which is already outlined. That one's about romantic relationships — specifically about why people who otherwise have good judgment tend to lose themselves completely when they fall in love.

I have a lot of material for that one.

For now though, I'm just going to sit with this for a day or two. Make a good coffee. Let the flatness pass.

It's done. That's enough for today.
